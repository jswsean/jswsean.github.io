Short Biography

My name is Sean Hambali. Currently, I work as a concurrent research assistant for the World Bank (Jakarta Office)
and the Institute for Economics and Social Research, Universitas Indonesia (LPEM-UI). I earned my B.E. from
Universitas Indonesia in 2018.


I have since worked with various clients and written several academic papers that have been published in
peer-reviewed journals. I have extensive experiences in data wrangling, visualization and statistical analyses;
specifically, I have developed a keen interest in studying causal inference.


Two of my most recently published works include "Is Motorcycle E-Hailing Welfare Improving?" and "Factors
explaining the expenditure of sports event participants and spectators: evidence from the 2018 Asian Games".
My most recent interactive visualization project on the long-term cost-benefits of Inpres school construction
(Akresh, Halim, Kleemans, 2021) can also be accessed here .


My research interests include development, digital and labor economics, or the intersection between the three
fields.
